Glossary of institutions

Explore the glossary

The glossary includes all of the institutions mentioned in the sources consulted. Although the list contains almost 650 entries, it is probably not complete. Descriptive data was collected for each institution, including the name and, where known, the date of its establishment and closure, etc.

The institutions have also been georeferenced, that is, assigned geographical coordinates, so that they can be located on the map. For each facility, original quotes have been gathered from the individual sources, which provide additional information on the purpose for which the facility was established and on the detainees held there.

All of the raw data is also available for downloading.

Glossary of legal texts

Explore the glossary

A large number of legal enactments (statutes, ordinances, regulations, etc.) were in effect both at the federal level and at the cantonal level, which provided the legal basis for decisions on administrative detention by the competent cantonal authorities.

Those enactments came from the domains of public law, civil law, or even criminal law, whereby a large number of the enactments fell within the domain of cantonal public law (cantonal administrative law). The laws focused on a wide range of individuals, who were characterised in different ways – e.g. as “dissolute or indolent”, “drunkards” (or “alcoholics”), juveniles, “mentally ill” (or persons with “psychological disorders”), “vagrants”, or “prostitutes”.
